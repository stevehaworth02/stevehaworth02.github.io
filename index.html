Clinical ML for EEG, Seizure Detection, and Consciousness Research
Steven Haworth
Master’s student in Data Science at UW–Madison focused on clinical and human-centered AI. My work centers on EEG-based machine learning for seizure detection, artifact rejection, and monitoring states of consciousness.

I design end-to-end ML pipelines that emphasize reliability, interpretability, and clinical usability, with the goal of building systems that can be trusted in real healthcare settings.
Current focus: end-to-end EEG ML pipelines (data curation → training → evaluation → deployment-ready tooling), with emphasis on robustness, calibration, and interpretable failure modes so outputs can be trusted in real workflows.

I’m open to research collaboration, clinical ML discussions, and opportunities that value both engineering rigor and impact.
